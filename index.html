Overview of Global Carbon Emissions (tCO₂/person, MtCO₂, kgCO₂/GDP)

Carbon emissions define the trajectory of climate change, but their sources are far from evenly distributed.

The map illustrates the stark differences in emissions across the globe. Countries like China, the United States, and India stand out as major contributors to global warming due to their industrialized economies and large populations. In contrast, smaller or less industrialized nations emit significantly less.

By selecting metrics such as emissions per capita, per GDP, or total emissions, the map unveils a layered understanding of these disparities. The time slider brings the evolution of emissions to life, showing how the global carbon landscape has shifted over decades.

The journey of carbon emissions over time reflects economic shifts, policy decisions, and global awareness.

The line chart tracks emissions from the mid-20th century to the present, revealing distinct patterns among nations. The United States, once a leading emitter, peaked in the late 20th century before stabilizing due to advances in technology and environmental policies. Meanwhile, China’s rapid industrialization in the 21st century propelled it to the forefront of global emissions, with India following a similar upward trajectory. Filters allow a closer look at specific countries or metrics like per capita emissions, enabling a deeper exploration of the factors shaping these trends. Each curve in the chart speaks to the challenges and progress of individual nations.

Carbon emissions are heavily concentrated in a handful of countries, shaping the planet’s climate challenges.

This ranking chart identifies the top emitters across different metrics. Countries like Qatar and the United States lead in emissions per capita, reflecting their energy consumption and economic structure, while nations like China dominate in total emissions. Adjusting the number, metric or year uncovers shifting dynamics among nations, highlighting how industrial growth, resource dependence, and environmental policies influence their standings. This visualization captures the scale of responsibility carried by leading emitters and the pivotal role they play in mitigating climate change.
